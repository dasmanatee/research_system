## North American Data Center Deals


Amid news that AI workloads areexpected to dominate data center buildoutswithin the next two years, this past month saw several high-profile data center announcements in the US.

AI powerhouseAnthropicunveiled a $50 billion planto build US data centers with UK-based cloud partnerFluidstack, starting with projects in New York and Texas. The companies said additional sites in the US are expected to follow and the project will create 800 permanent jobs and 2,000 construction roles.

Meanwhile,GoogleandWestinghousehave joined forcesto optimize the construction and operation of 10 planned nuclear reactorsslated to begin construction in 2030. The companies said they have been working for months on a plan that boosts normally sluggish nuclear reactor construction timelines with a custom AI-driven Google Cloud optimization platform.

Metais eyeing a steady stream of power bywading into the complex world of electricity trading, betting it can accelerate the construction of new US power plants that are vital to its AI ambitions. The decision comes after Meta heard from investors and plant developers that too few power buyers were willing to make the early, long-term commitments required to spur investment

In Wisconsin, the Facebook parent companyis set to spend more than $1 billion to build an AI data center. The 700,000 sq.ft facility will be constructed in Beaver Dam, a small city north of Madison and Milwaukee.

Elsewhere in the state,OpenAIandFoxconnannounced a collaborationto focus on design work and US manufacturing readiness for next-gen AI infrastructure hardware. According to a joint statement from the companies, the rapid advancement of AI necessitates a new class of physical infrastructure.

Energy Storage Solutionscleared a major hurdlein its plan to build a massive 900 MW, 300-acre data center and energy storage campus in eastern North Carolina after county commissioners added data center construction to permitted zoning. Dan Shaffer, president of the Rocky Mount-based company, toldData Center Knowledgethat the $19.2 billion project will begin construction in the first quarter of 2026. A twin project is also planned for Fayetteville.

There was a flurry of activity in Virginia asVantage Data Centersexpanded its footprintin the state with a new $2 billion investment. The new 192 MW campus in the Fredericksburg region will bring the company’s statewide capacity to 782 MW.

Elsewhere in the Mother of States,CleanArc Data Centersbegan construction on its flagship campusin Caroline County, Virginia. The new data center campus will provide 900 MW of grid capacity, with the first 300MW available by early 2027.

Lastly, Richmond suburb Powhatan County unanimouslyapproved the development of a new 180-acre data center, according to real estate and investment companyJLL, which petitioned the County Board.Dominion Energyplans to  power the campus with an initial deployment of 365 MW.

In Atlanta,Microsoftannounced what it calls the first AI superfactory, linking multiple facilities within its Fairwater project with hundreds of thousands of advanced GPUs running AI workloads, exabytes of storage, and millions of CPU cores for operational compute tasks.

## European Data Center Developments


InEurope, major power outages have led antitrust watchdogsto consider placing new restrictions on Big Tech. The European Commission is investigating the market power ofAmazon,Microsoft, andGoogle, which have thus far avoided the full extent of Europe’s regulatory laws. If implemented, the tech leaders could see steep penalties.

Coming as the Dutch data center market experiences rapid growth,Digital Realtylaunched its 12th data centerin the Netherlands. AMS11 will add approximately 27 MW of IT capacity across 12,000 sq.m, increasing the company’s total capacity in the country to around 159 MW.

In Germany,Googleplans to invest €5.5 billion into computing resources and operationsover the next four years. That development includes two facilities in the Frankfurt region.

UK-based data center companyAtlasEdgewill utilize $292 million in sustainability-linked fundingto develop two data centers at its new campus in Lisbon, Portugal. The funding, backed by Banco Santander and ING, requires energy efficiency and 100% renewable energy usage.

In Spain,Qilimanjaro Quantum Techhas opened what it claims isEurope’s first multimodal quantum-powered data center,preparing to offer cloud-based quantum computing as a service. The facility will house up to 10 quantum computers.

Spanish engineering firmACSandBlackRockwill collaborate on a new data center projectwith the aim of developing 1.7 GW of capacity. BlackRock agreed to about half of the €2 billion upfront and the other according to milestones, with each company owning 50% of the new venture.Iberdrolaand data center developerEchelon Data Centreshave partnered on a new companywith planned investments of more than €2 billion. The joint venture’s first announced project isMadrid Sur, a 160,000 m² campus offering 144 MW of processing capacity with a secured 230 MW electricity connection.

Further north,atNorthhasreached an agreementwithVestforbrændingto use excess heat from its DEN01 facility for heating over 8,000 homes in Denmark by 2028. The 22.5 MW site will channel water using Direct Liquid Cooling technology into the district heating network.

The announcement came amidreportsthat Digital Realty and Equinix were vying to acquire atNorth.

In Norway,a new AI infrastructure startup launchedcalledFossefall. The company laid out objectives on its LinkedIn page including developing 500 MW of clean AI infrastructure by 2030, generating in-country jobs, and making a positive impact on the environment.


## Asia-Pacific Data Centers Builds


InAsia-Pacificdata center news, Malaysia’s palm oil companies aresetting aside vast tracts of land for new AI data center construction. That land will be needed to house the infrastructure for rapidly growing power demands, with one estimate stating data centers will consume 5GW of electricity in the country by 2035.

In Vietnam, real estate developerKinh Bac City Development Holding Corporationhassigned a memorandum of agreementwithAccelerated Infrastructure CapitalandVietinBankto jointly develop a 200 MW AI data centerproject in Ho Chi Minh City.

DayOne Data CentersandAmata Corporationplan to develop a new park and expand an existing data center campus, building Thailand’s first 1GW power platform. The memorandum of agreement outlined the allotment of 16 additional acres of land, bringing the CTP1 site’s total capacity to 300 MW.

In Australia, New South Walesapproved a $3.1 billion data centercampus to be constructed byCDC Data Centersin Marsden Park. This will be the southern hemisphere’s largest data center project.

Three developers in western Sydney –Starwood Capital Group,Doma Infrastructure GroupandTelstra InfraCo–inked an agreement to jointly develop a 62MW data center. The groups secured approval for the site in Minchinbury and expect to complete the data center by early 2028.

In Middle East and Africa data center news,the UAE committed $1 billionfor a new AI for Development initiative to support and finance artificial intelligence projects in African countries. The Abu Dhabi Exports Office (ADEX), a division of the Abu Dhabi Fund for Development, will collaborate with the UAE Foreign Aid Agency on the project.

As Abu Dhabi aims to become the world’s first AI-native government,Oracleannounced a major expansion in the regionwith the deployment of its first OCI supercluster, powered by over 4,000NvidiaBlackwell GPUs.

The UAE AI industry ispoised to receive an injection of $15 billionover the next four years fromMicrosoft, with the investment intended to support the country's AI and cloud infrastructure.
